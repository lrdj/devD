What are five things that matter?

Good teams have principles, and good businesses have purpose. What are the things you carry with you as you grow across engagements? What do you try and develop in design teams?

1. Doing it together

Foster collaboration in the studio and with other disciplines beyond. Individual culture and identity do matter, but assholes scale and ego gets in the way of growth. If design is on a journey, we're going to go further by valuing the energy of our collective learning and output.

2. Iteration

Not being afraid of making mistakes is hard. Learning from them can seem even harder. We all need a culture that empowers our team members to experiment without the fear of failure, and it's a massive part of a design leader's role to enable and nurture that environment.

4. Consistent vision

A consistent vision allows design solutions to share common values. Design leaders bring "a way of solving it" and this provides a stable foundation for design teams who then know what good looks like.

5. Mentorship

Leadership can involve influence, but I'd hope designers speak of the learning they received, emphasising the role of the leader as a guide and mentor.

6. Holistic Thinking

A design challenge lives within a larger ecosystem. Teams need the permission to explore the broader context, ensuring that designs become a synthesis of business and user needs.

7. Empowering Exploration

Leaders need to find creative ways to foster environments where exploration is encouraged. We should be allowing teams to push boundaries and venture into learning spaces that may not immediately provide tangible results.

8. Learning

Beyond his immediate team, Breuer's work at institutions like the Bauhaus laid down pedagogical frameworks that have trained and inspired countless designers. His leadership was thus not confined to his direct team but extended to generations of designers who came after him.

Can you give us an example where you’ve helped an organisation to increase their design maturity?

Most people aren’t trained to want to face the process of re-understanding a subject they already know. One must obtain not just literacy, but deep involvement and re-understanding.
